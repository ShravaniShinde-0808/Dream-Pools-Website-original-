How they work: Water is passed through a tank filled with sand, which traps debris and particles. Pros: Cost-effective, low maintenance, and suitable for larger pools. Cons: Not as effective at removing smaller particles as other filter types. Maintenance: Sand needs to be replaced every 3-5 years, according to Mentor Water Experts, and backwashing (cleaning) is required periodically.

How they work: Water passes through a pleated filter cartridge, which traps debris. Pros: Offer finer filtration than sand filters, easy to clean, and compact. Cons: Cartridges need to be replaced periodically, which can be an ongoing expense. Maintenance: Cartridges are cleaned by removing and hosing them down, and need to be replaced when they become clogged.

How they work: DE powder, made from fossilized diatoms, coats grids or tubes inside the filter, trapping very fine particles. Pros: Provide the highest level of water clarity and filtration. Cons: Require more maintenance and can be more expensive to operate. Maintenance: DE needs to be added to the filter regularly, and the grids need to be cleaned periodically.
